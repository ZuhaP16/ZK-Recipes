Welcome to the UAE's Culinary World

The United Arab Emirates is known for its deep-rooted traditions, warm hospitality, and a cuisine that reflects its rich heritage. Emirati food is simple yet hearty, with dishes that have been passed down for generations. Let’s explore one of the UAE’s most treasured traditional dishes!

National Dish: Al Harees

What is Al Harees?

Al Harees is a traditional Emirati dish made with just a few ingredients yet packed with deep, comforting flavors. It consists of wheat, meat (usually chicken or lamb), and a pinch of salt, slow-cooked for hours until it reaches a smooth, porridge-like consistency. The result is a rich, wholesome dish often enjoyed during Ramadan and festive occasions.

History and Origins

Al Harees dates back centuries and has remained an essential part of Emirati culture. Known for its simplicity and nourishing qualities, it was originally prepared in clay pots over wood fires, requiring patience and care. Traditionally served in large gatherings, Al Harees symbolizes generosity, unity, and the strong sense of community in the UAE.
