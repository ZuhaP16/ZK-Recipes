SALEEG

Taste of the Gulf

Prep Time: 15 mins

Cook Time:45 mins

Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 2 cups short-grain rice (e.g., arborio or saleeg rice)
  • 4 cups chicken broth or water
  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces (or 4 chicken thighs)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp ghee or butter
  • 1/4 cup toasted pine nuts or almonds (optional, for garnish)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Steps

  1. In a large pot, heat the ghee or butter over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic and sauté until softened and fragrant.
  2. Add the chicken pieces to the pot and cook until browned on all sides.
  3. Sprinkle in the cumin, cinnamon, turmeric, black pepper, and salt. Stir to coat the chicken in the spices.
  4. Pour in the chicken broth (or water) and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat, cover, and simmer for 25-30 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and tender.
  5. While the chicken is cooking, rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear.
  6. Once the chicken is cooked, remove it from the pot and set it aside. Add the rinsed rice to the pot with the broth and spices. Stir gently to combine.
  7. Cover the pot and cook the rice on low heat for 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is tender and has absorbed most of the liquid. If needed, add more water or broth to ensure the rice cooks properly.
  8. Once the rice is cooked, fluff it with a fork. Shred or chop the cooked chicken and stir it back into the rice, mixing well.
  9. Garnish with toasted pine nuts or almonds and fresh parsley before serving.
