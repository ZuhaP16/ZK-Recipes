MUTABBAQ

Taste of the Gulf

Prep Time: 20 mins

Cook Time:30 mins

Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 2 tbsp ghee or vegetable oil
  • 1/2 lb ground beef or chicken (optional)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon (optional)
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Oil for frying

Steps

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine flour, salt, sugar, and warm water. Knead until you form a smooth dough. Cover and let it rest for 15 minutes.
  2. While the dough is resting, heat 2 tbsp of oil or ghee in a pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic and sauté until soft and translucent.
  3. Add the ground beef or chicken to the pan and cook until browned. Season with cumin, coriander, turmeric, cinnamon, and salt. Stir to combine, and cook for a few more minutes until the mixture is well seasoned.
  4. Remove the filling from the pan and let it cool slightly. If the mixture is too dry, you can add a little bit of water to create a moist filling. Set aside.
  5. Divide the rested dough into small balls. Roll each ball into a thin round sheet using a rolling pin, as thin as you can without tearing the dough.
  6. Place a portion of the filling in the center of each dough sheet, then fold the edges over to form a square or rectangle, sealing the filling inside.
  7. Heat oil in a large skillet or griddle over medium heat. Once hot, place the mutabbaq in the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, pressing gently to flatten it and ensure the filling is cooked through.
  8. Once golden and crispy on both sides, remove the mutabbaq from the pan and place it on a paper towel to absorb any excess oil.
  9. Cut into squares and serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley and accompanied by a side of yogurt or salad.
