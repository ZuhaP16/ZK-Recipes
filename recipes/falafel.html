FALAFEL

Taste of the Gulf

Prep Time: 15 mins

Cook Time:25 mins

Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas (or 2 cups canned, drained)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2-3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2-1 tsp dried hot pepper
  • 4-6 tbsp flour (or more if needed)
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • Oil for frying

Steps

  1. Place dried chickpeas in a bowl and cover with cold water. Let them soak for 12 hours or overnight. Drain well.
  2. Place the chickpeas, onion, garlic, parsley, cilantro, cumin, coriander, salt, and pepper in a food processor. Pulse until the mixture is finely chopped but not pureed.
  3. Sprinkle the baking powder and 4 tablespoons of flour over the mixture and pulse again. If the dough is sticky, add more flour a tablespoon at a time until it holds together.
  4. Cover the dough and refrigerate for several hours or overnight.
  5. Form the dough into small balls or patties (about 1 1/2 inches in diameter) and set them aside.
  6. Heat 3 inches of oil to 375°F (190°C) in a deep fryer or large pan. Fry the falafel in batches until golden brown and crisp, about 4-5 minutes per batch.
  7. Drain on paper towels and serve hot with pita bread, tahini, or a yogurt-based sauce.
