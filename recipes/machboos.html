MACHBOOS

Taste of the Gulf

Prep Time: 20 mins

Cook Time: 1 hour 30 mins

Servings: 4-6

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 kg mutton (bone-in, cut into chunks)
  • 2 onions (finely chopped)
  • 3 tomatoes (chopped)
  • 4 garlic cloves (minced)
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 3 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 4 cardamom pods
  • 4 cloves
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp coriander
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp chili powder (optional)
  • 1 tsp baharat spice blend
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 cups water or mutton broth
  • 1/4 cup raisins (optional, for garnish)
  • 1/4 cup toasted almonds (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Rinse the basmati rice under cold water and soak for 30 minutes, then drain.
  2. In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add garlic and all the whole spices (cinnamon stick, cardamom, cloves, and bay leaf). Stir for 1 minute.
  4. Add the mutton pieces and brown them on all sides.
  5. Stir in the chopped tomatoes and tomato paste. Cook for 5 minutes until softened.
  6. Add cumin, coriander, turmeric, black pepper, chili powder, baharat, and salt. Mix well.
  7. Pour in the water or mutton broth and bring to a boil. Cover and let simmer for 1 hour, or until the mutton is tender.
  8. Remove the mutton pieces and set them aside.
  9. Add the soaked basmati rice to the pot, stirring gently.
  10. Return the mutton on top of the rice, cover, and cook on low heat for 20 minutes.
  11. Once done, fluff the rice and garnish with raisins and toasted almonds.
  12. Serve hot with yogurt or salad on the side.
