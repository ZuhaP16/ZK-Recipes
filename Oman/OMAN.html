Welcome to Oman's Culinary World

Omani cuisine is a reflection of the country's rich history, hospitality, and love for deeply flavored dishes. With a mix of spices and slow-cooked delicacies, Oman's traditional food is both comforting and full of heritage. Let’s discover one of the most special dishes in Oman!

National Dish: Shuwa

What is Shuwa?

Shuwa is a traditional Omani dish known for its slow-cooked, tender meat infused with rich spices. Typically made with lamb or goat, the meat is marinated in a blend of Omani spices, wrapped in banana or palm leaves, and then cooked in an underground sand oven for up to 48 hours. The result is an incredibly juicy, flavorful dish enjoyed on festive occasions.

History and Origins

Shuwa has been an essential part of Omani culture for centuries, traditionally prepared for celebrations such as Eid and weddings. Cooking the meat underground is a method passed down through generations, symbolizing patience and community spirit. The long cooking process allows the flavors to deepen, making Shuwa a prized dish that represents Omani hospitality and tradition.
