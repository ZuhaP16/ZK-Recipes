National Dish: Machboos

What is Machboos?

Machboos, Kuwait's national dish, is a fragrant rice-based delicacy celebrated for its hearty and flavorful profile. It features basmati rice cooked with tender meat, often chicken or lamb, and a blend of aromatic spices such as turmeric, saffron, and cardamom. The dish is typically accompanied by a tangy tomato sauce called "daqqous" and garnished with fried onions, nuts, and dried fruits for added texture and richness.

History and Origins

Machboos reflects Kuwait's maritime heritage and its connection to trade routes that brought exotic spices to the Gulf. Rooted in traditional Bedouin and coastal cuisine, this dish symbolizes the essence of Kuwaiti hospitality and culture. It has become a centerpiece at family gatherings, festivals, and special occasions, representing the unity and warmth of Kuwaiti traditions.
